The Meaning of "Do or do not. There is no 'Try'"

May 4, 2023

Master Yoda’s quote, “Do or do not, there is no try,” is one of those lines that sticks with people long after they’ve heard it. At first, it might seem a little harsh—like it’s saying failure isn’t allowed—but it’s actually not about that at all. It’s more about how you approach things. If you go into something with a half-hearted attitude, already thinking, “I’ll try, but I’ll probably fail,” you’re setting yourself up to fall short.

Think about times in your life when you really wanted something. Maybe it was learning a new skill, getting in shape, or tackling a big project. The times you’ve succeeded are probably when you threw yourself into it 100%. You didn’t just “try.” You decided, “I’m doing this,” and put everything you had into it. That’s what Yoda’s talking about. He’s not saying you won’t stumble or fail along the way, but that full commitment is what gives you the best shot at success.

In lightsaber training, for example, this lesson is crucial. When you’re learning a difficult technique, if you only put in half the effort because you think you might not get it, you’re limiting yourself. But if you go all in—trusting in your ability, focusing completely—you’re much more likely to make progress, even if you don’t get it right the first time. That’s because full commitment sharpens your focus and opens you up to learning from your mistakes rather than letting them hold you back.

This idea translates to everyday life too. Let’s say you’ve got a big goal, like starting a new career or improving your health. If you approach it thinking, “I’ll give it a shot, but who knows?” you’re likely to hit a wall sooner than later. On the other hand, if you decide, “I’m going to make this happen,” and throw yourself into the work—no matter how tough it gets—you’re more likely to keep pushing through the challenges.

“Do or do not, there is no try” is really about mindset. It’s not about guaranteeing success every time, but about showing up fully. It’s about giving yourself the best chance to succeed by believing in what you’re doing and not holding back. Whether you’re learning to wield a lightsaber, taking on a personal challenge, or chasing a dream, going all in with focus and commitment makes all the difference.

And remember: the Force will be with you. Always.

- Nathan J. Boquiren
